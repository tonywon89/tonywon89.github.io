ABOUT MYSELF

My name is Tony Won and I am a software developer. But I didn't start out that way. In fact, I spent most of my life pursuing a different career path: veterinary medicine. I even got into the top veterinary school at the time, Cornell University, and attended for two years (out of four) before I changed paths. Since then, I have never looked back, and couldn't be happier.


I first discovered the joy of creating through playing Minecraft, where I spent hours building grand castles and advanced mining mechanisms. After that, I wanted to translate this new found passion into something more practical, so I spent a summer learning computer programming through various online courses, such as Codecademy and Standford's CS106A: Programming Methodology. I built my own Android app (which I still have on my phone to this day) and my first full-stack web application using Ruby on Rails: WorkToRelax. I have been hooked to coding since.


To turn coding into a career, I attended App Academy, a 1000-hour intensive web development course, where I learned not only about core concepts such as object-oriented programming and the underlying mechanism of Rails's ActiveRecord, but also how to write clean code and implement neat data structures (trees, hash maps, LRU caches). It was great surrounding myself with motivated individuals, all with the same drive and passion for coding. At the end of each day, I would reflect back and be amazed at how much I enjoyed coding, and how excited I am about the next day.


I am looking forward to working in a company where I can not only contribute my skills to create great websites, but also learn and grow as a developer.
